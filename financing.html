How to Finance Padel Courts

Most padel court projects in Ireland use a combination of grants and financing. Here's what you need to know about funding options.

Available Grants in Ireland

Grant funding can significantly reduce your investment. Many projects secure €30,000-70,000 before arranging any loans.

Community Sport Facilities Fund

The main grant for sports equipment in Ireland. Previously called the Sports Capital Programme.

  • • Up to €70,000 for equipment
  • • 95% funding rate
  • • Annual application windows
  • • €230 million allocated in 2024

Sport Ireland Funding

Various programs supporting sports development and infrastructure.

  • • €31.3 million budget (2025)
  • • Dormant Accounts Fund available
  • • Priority for underserved areas

LEADER Programme

For rural community projects and facilities.

  • • Variable amounts
  • • Rural areas only
  • • Applied through Local Action Groups

Common Financing Questions

What's the typical investment for padel courts?

A single court typically ranges from €35,000-60,000 depending on the model and site requirements. Most clubs start with 2-4 courts. Groundworks can add 30-40% to the total project cost.

Do I need to pay everything upfront?

No. Most projects use a combination of grants and financing. Typical structure might be: 30-40% from grants, 60-70% financed over 3-7 years. Many clubs start generating revenue before construction is complete through memberships and pre-bookings.

What do lenders look for?

Lenders typically want to see: a solid business plan, suitable location, some industry experience (sports/hospitality helps), and realistic projections. The strong growth in padel makes it attractive to lenders familiar with the sport.

Can the courts pay for themselves?

Well-located padel courts typically achieve 50-70% utilization within 12-18 months. At standard rates (€40-60/hour), this generates strong cash flow. Most operators report break-even within 24-36 months.

Should I buy or lease?

Both options work. Buying (through asset finance) builds equity and offers more control. Leasing keeps it off your balance sheet and may have lower monthly payments. Your accountant can advise what's best for your situation.
